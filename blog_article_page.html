Top 10 Nutritious Snacks for Your Tabby

Sarah Whiskers
Vet Nutritionist • Oct 24, 2023 • 5 min read

We all love to spoil our feline friends, but finding the right balance between "tasty" and "healthy" can be a cat-and-mouse game. Today, we're diving into the pantry to find the best nutritious snacks that won't compromise your tabby's waistline.

It's 3 AM. You hear the distinct sound of a paw batting at a plastic wrapper. Your cat is on the hunt, and unfortunately, they've found the bag of potato chips you left on the counter. While our furry companions might be curious about human food, their dietary needs are strictly carnivorous.

Why Nutrition Matters for Snacking

Just like humans, cats can suffer from obesity, diabetes, and heart disease if their diet isn't managed correctly. Treats should make up no more than 10% of a cat's daily caloric intake. The rest should come from a balanced, high-quality cat food.

"The biggest mistake owners make is equating food with love. Playtime is a much better reward than a third helping of treats." — Dr. Whiskers

1. Cooked Chicken

Plain, cooked chicken is a fantastic source of lean protein. Make sure it's boneless and skinless to avoid choking hazards and excess fat.

2. Blueberries

Surprisingly, many cats enjoy the texture of frozen blueberries. They are packed with antioxidants and vitamins A and C. Plus, watching them bat a frozen berry across the floor is free entertainment.

warning Toxic Foods to Avoid

Never feed your cat onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, chocolate, or caffeine. These can be fatal even in small amounts.

3. Pumpkin Puree

Not the pie filling with spices! Plain canned pumpkin is excellent for digestion and can help with hairballs due to its fiber content.

4. Cat Grass

Oat grass or wheatgrass is a safe way to satisfy your cat's instinct to graze. It aids digestion and is a fun indoor gardening project.

The Verdict

Remember, every cat is an individual. What one tabby loves, another might turn their nose up at. Introduce new treats slowly and in small portions to see how their stomach reacts.
